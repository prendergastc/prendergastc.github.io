Spatial Analysis

The overall goal of the presentation is to demonstrate one example of a more complex real-life spatial analysis workflow from start to finish. The goal of the specific analysis chosen for this demonstration is to understand how the Supreme Court decision which overturned Roe v. Wade impacts access to women’s healthcare across the country. This analysis illustrates how decisions made at the national and global levels can have varied impacts on a local scale. In this case, the specific impact analyzed is the loss of access to women’s healthcare, specifically the increases in travel times to access these services. It also examines the potential increase in demand at the remaining women’s clinics. Finally, this analysis examines the spatial distribution of the cumulative burden created by these changes.

Feature to Point vs Mean Center Tools

The feature to point and mean center tools are used to create representative points for area features such as census tracts. Each tool has pros and cons. On one hand, the feature to point tool is simple to use, quick to run, and easy to understand. However, the centroids it creates for areas such as census tracts may not be representative of where the population actually live. This is especially true for larger, less densely populated, rural census tracts. On the other hand, the mean center tool uses a population weighted calculation to give a more representative location for where the bulk of the population is located in each census tract. This calculation is based on the more granular population data available at the census block level from the Living Atlas. One downside of the mean center tool is that for large datasets it can take a considerable amount of time to run.

Multivariate Clustering

In this analysis the multivariate clustering tool was used to uncover spatial patterns in clusters of census tracts which share similar scores on a multivariate index representing the cumulative burden of several factors. Under the hood the multivariate clustering tool uses the k-means clustering method.

Conclusion

My main take away from the video was that the two women presenters care deeply about this issue which will have a profound impact on access to women’s health care across the nation. The analysis illustrates that the main impact will be increased travel time for women and increased demand at the remaining location which continue to provide women’s healthcare services. The spatial analysis highlights that these impacts will not be distributed evenly across space. Moreover, the analysis shows that the cumulative burden will fall disproportionately on communities that are already disadvantaged and marginalized. The map presented illustrates that residents of Texas will be especially heavily impacted.
